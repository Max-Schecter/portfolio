PROVE - Aeroshell Allignment

  • Design Requirements:

    • The vehicle Aeroshell and chassis must be aligned with high precision in order to avoid interference.
    • The Aeroshell must be centered on the vehicle's track width.
    • The location method cannot use any points on the vehicle chassis, as they may be out of tolerence.

  • Method:

    • I began by analyzing the closest point of interference, as that would determine the required precision.
    • I considered measurment methods like 3d scanners, tape measures, and string, weighting the pros and cons of each method based on cost, tolerence stackup, and space required.

  • Solution:

    • Project the bottom panel mounting holes onto a 2d plane and use three laser measurers to triangulate their positions, including the track width centerline.
    • The bottom panel’s precise placement will serve as the base for building the entire aeroshell.
    • Since the bottom pannel only exists in 2d space, and there is an extra laser measurer, we will avoid instense tolerence stackup.

PROVE - Charging Port

  • Design Requirements:

    • Must seamlessly mate with the vehicle's organic geometry.
    • Must house a J1772 charging plug.
    • Must be easy to manufacture.

  • Method:

    • I chose the rear-left location for access, proximity to high-voltage parts, and available space.
    • I reviewed existing designs, opting for a custom hinged design due to complex vehicle geometry.
    • I modeled the part in Siemens NX, designing the part for effecient 3d printing.

  • Results:

    • I 3D printed prototypes to confirm manufacturability and functionality. The final part will be printed in PETG.
    • I'm waiting on the completion of the vehicle Aeroshell to integrate it and perform final tests.

PROVE - Headlights and Tail Lights

  • Design Requirements:

    • Must match the asthetic design of the vehicle.
    • Must be able to house a five inch deep projector headlight and fit within tight geometry constraints.
    • The tail light bar must also act as a structural member, holding two pannels together.

  • Method:

    • I began by sketching out initial headlight designs on the profile of the vehicle.
    • I then performed intial exploratory design work in the headlight in Siemens NX.
    • I also performed initial design work on the Tail light assembly.

  • Results/Next Steps:

    • I decided to go with a split headlight design and am continuing design work on it. The housing will be 3D printed, and the lens will be vacuum-formed acrylic.
    • I scrapped the tail light bar, making it a purely structural member due to high costs and manufacturing challenges.
    • I'm finalizing the CAD models and will 3D print a prototype soon.
